We spent 3 weeks building an "autonomous AI agent" for a client.

Turned out it was:
• 80% hardcoded rules
• 15% API calls  
• 5% actual AI

Here's what we learned about the AI agent hype:

**1. The autonomy illusion**

Most "agents" are just deterministic workflows with a chat interface. They follow pre-defined paths, handle known cases, and break on anything unexpected.

Not autonomous. Just automated.

**2. Where AI actually helps**

After building 6+ "agents" for clients, the pattern is clear:

✅ Great at: Pattern recognition, content generation, summarization
❌ Bad at: Decision-making, edge cases, accountability

The best AI work we've done? Research summarization that saves 10hrs/week. Zero decision-making. Pure value.

**3. The hidden complexity**

Client wanted an agent to handle customer support.

Reality:
• 80% of queries → handled by rules
• 20% of queries → escalated to humans
• 100% of edge cases → needed human oversight

We built a workflow, not an agent. And that's okay.

**4. When to use agents vs scripts**

Our decision framework:

→ Need judgment calls? Use humans + AI assistance
→ Repetitive pattern work? AI alone works
→ Need accountability? Human in the loop
→ Just moving data? Plain automation is cheaper

**5. The honest conversation**

Now we tell clients upfront:

"You probably don't need an AI agent. You need a workflow with AI at specific points."

90% of the value. 10% of the complexity.

**The takeaway**

AI agents aren't fake. But the hype is.

Most "autonomous" systems today are sophisticated scripts. And that's fine — scripts solve real problems.

Just call them what they are.
