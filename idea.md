## Core Concept

Everyone's talking about AI agents replacing entire workflows. The reality? Most "autonomous agents" today are just glorified if-then scripts with a chat interface.

## Hook

"We spent 3 weeks building an 'autonomous AI agent' for a client. Turned out it was 80% hardcoded rules, 15% API calls, and 5% actual AI. Here's what we learned..."

## Key Points

1. **The autonomy illusion** — Most "agents" are deterministic workflows pretending to be intelligent
2. **Where AI actually helps** — Pattern recognition, content generation, summarization (not decision-making)
3. **The hidden complexity** — Edge cases, error handling, and human oversight still required
4. **When to use agents vs scripts** — Decision framework for when AI adds real value
5. **The honest approach** — Why we tell clients "you don't need an agent, you need a workflow"

## Real Examples

- **Agent that failed:** Customer support "agent" that couldn't handle 20% of queries → escalated to humans anyway
- **Agent that worked:** Research summarization → saved 10hrs/week, no decision-making needed
- **Better alternative:** Simple automation + AI for specific tasks → 90% of the value, 10% of the complexity
